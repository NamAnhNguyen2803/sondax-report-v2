### April 2026 Regulation — exactly what changed

Issued 13 April 2026 by **Tuyen Quang Province's Department of Culture, Sports and Tourism** (note: the 2025–2026 provincial reorganisation means Ha Giang is now administered under Tuyen Quang's umbrella in places). [verified — VnExpress International]

**New requirements:**
1. All drivers and vehicles must fully comply with road safety regulations (spot-check enforcement).
2. Motorbikes must be properly maintained + fitted with helmets + essential protective equipment.
3. Service providers must have **legally binding contracts with travel agencies** specifying drivers, vehicles, routes, rest stops, incident responsibilities.
4. **Unlicensed operators prohibited** from independently organising or selling tours.
5. Transport providers cannot unilaterally alter itineraries, replace drivers, or modify passenger arrangements outside agreed contracts.
6. Authorities to organise training programs for transport businesses.

**Standing license rules (unchanged but now enforced strictly):**
- Foreign self-drivers must hold valid home-country licence **AND 1968 Vienna Convention IDP**.
- Fines if caught: VND 2–4M (<125cc) / VND 6–8M (>125cc).
- Checkpoints in Ha Giang City, Đồng Văn, Mèo Vạc.
[verified — looptrails.com, mrbiuhagiangloop.com, bongbackpackerhostel.com]

### Current state post-April 2026 (as of 2026-04-20)

- Regulation enforcement ramp-up. Operators without formal travel-agency contracts at risk.
- Short-term: expect -10 to -20% bookings for budget/hostel tier via trust shock [estimate — tourism-shock literature].
- Medium-term (Q3 2026+): premium-safety tier structural winner; cost-to-operate rises for all (compliance + insurance).
- USA, Vietnam's passage of 1968 Vienna Convention is a hard gate: ~60% of US travellers, who issue IDPs under 1949 Geneva, are technically non-compliant for self-drive unless they get a VN local licence. Easy Rider / car tour substitution expected.
